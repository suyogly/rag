## 32. Finance and Expense Management

Financial discipline is a foundational requirement for the sustainability of Sagarmatha Systems. Employees are expected to exercise judgment and responsibility when incurring expenses on behalf of the company.

All business-related expenses must be pre-approved by the relevant manager unless they fall under predefined categories of minor operational costs. Expenses incurred without prior approval may not be reimbursed unless exceptional justification is provided.

Expenses must be submitted through the designated expense management system within seven working days of being incurred. Each submission must include appropriate documentation, such as receipts or invoices, along with a clear description of the purpose of the expense.

Reimbursements are processed within a standard cycle of ten working days following approval. Delays may occur if documentation is incomplete or if additional verification is required.

Common reimbursable expenses include travel costs for official purposes, work-related software or tools approved in advance, and operational necessities required for task completion. Personal expenses, even if incurred during work hours, are not eligible for reimbursement.

The finance team reserves the right to audit expenses periodically to ensure compliance with company policies.

## 33. Procurement Process

Procurement of equipment, software, or services must follow a structured process to ensure cost efficiency and compatibility with organizational needs.

All procurement requests must be submitted through the internal system, specifying the required item, justification, estimated cost, and intended use. Requests are reviewed by the relevant manager and, where applicable, the finance team.

For purchases above NPR 50,000, at least two quotations must be obtained to ensure competitive pricing. Exceptions may be made for specialized items where alternative suppliers are not available.

Approved purchases are executed by the operations or finance team to maintain consistency and proper record-keeping. Employees are generally not authorized to make direct purchases on behalf of the company unless explicitly approved.

All procured assets are recorded in the company’s asset registry and assigned to the relevant employee or team. Employees are responsible for maintaining the condition and security of assigned assets.

## 34. Travel Policy

Travel may be required for business purposes, including client meetings, training, or inter-office coordination. All travel must be approved in advance by the employee’s manager.

Travel arrangements, including transportation and accommodation, should be made through the company’s designated channels to ensure cost control and consistency.

Employees are expected to choose reasonable and cost-effective options when traveling. Luxury accommodations or premium travel options are not permitted unless explicitly justified and approved.

Per diem allowances may be provided for meals and incidental expenses during travel. The rates are defined by the finance team and communicated periodically.

Travel-related expenses must be documented and submitted in accordance with the expense management policy.

Employees are expected to represent the company professionally during travel and adhere to all relevant policies and local regulations.

---

## 35. Remote Work and Equipment Usage

Sagarmatha Systems supports a limited remote work policy, allowing employees to work remotely for up to two days per week, subject to managerial approval and operational requirements.

Employees working remotely are expected to maintain the same level of productivity, availability, and communication as they would in the office. Reliable internet connectivity and a suitable work environment are required.

Company-provided equipment, including laptops and peripherals, must be used primarily for work-related purposes. Employees are responsible for ensuring the security and proper maintenance of these assets.

Loss, damage, or theft of company equipment must be reported immediately. Replacement or repair costs may be assessed depending on the circumstances.

Software installed on company devices must comply with licensing and security requirements. Unauthorized software installations are not permitted.

## 36. Information Security and Confidentiality

Information security is a shared responsibility across the organization. Employees must adhere to established security practices to protect company and client data.

Confidential information includes, but is not limited to, proprietary code, system architecture, client data, financial records, and internal communications. Such information must not be disclosed to unauthorized parties.

Employees are required to use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication on all systems where it is supported. Passwords must not be shared under any circumstances.

Access to systems and data is granted based on role and necessity. Employees must not attempt to access information beyond their authorized scope.

Any suspected security incidents, including unauthorized access or data breaches, must be reported immediately to the designated security contact.

Failure to comply with security policies may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

## 37. Legal Compliance and Intellectual Property

Sagarmatha Systems operates in compliance with applicable laws and regulations in Nepal and other jurisdictions where it conducts business.

Employees are expected to perform their duties in a manner that complies with all relevant legal requirements. Any concerns regarding legal compliance should be raised with the operations or legal team.

All work produced by employees in the course of their employment, including code, designs, documentation, and other materials, is the intellectual property of Sagarmatha Systems.

Employees must not use company resources to develop products or services that compete with the company’s business interests. Any external work must be disclosed and approved if there is a potential conflict of interest.

Upon termination of employment, employees are required to return all company property and cease use of any proprietary information.

---

## 38. Conflict of Interest

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with the interests of the company.

A conflict of interest may arise if an employee has a financial or personal relationship with a client, vendor, or competitor that could influence their decision-making.

Employees are required to disclose any potential conflicts of interest to their manager or the operations team. Failure to disclose such conflicts may result in disciplinary action.

The company will assess disclosed conflicts and determine appropriate measures, which may include reassignment of responsibilities or other actions.

---

## 39. Termination of Employment

Employment at Sagarmatha Systems may be terminated by either the employee or the company, subject to the terms of the employment agreement.

Employees are required to provide a minimum notice period of 30 days when resigning. The company may waive this requirement at its discretion.

Termination by the company may occur due to performance issues, behavioral concerns, redundancy, or other legitimate reasons. In such cases, the company will follow applicable laws and internal procedures.

In cases of serious misconduct, termination may be immediate without notice.

Final settlements, including salary, leave balance, and other dues, are processed in accordance with company policy and legal requirements.

---

## 40. Exit Process

The exit process is designed to ensure a smooth transition and proper closure of responsibilities.

Employees are required to complete a knowledge transfer process, documenting their work and handing over responsibilities to designated individuals.

All company assets, including equipment, access credentials, and documents, must be returned before the final working day.

An exit interview may be conducted to gather feedback on the employee’s experience. Participation is encouraged but not mandatory.

Access to company systems is revoked upon completion of the exit process or earlier if required.

## 41. Health, Safety, and Workplace Environment

Sagarmatha Systems is committed to providing a safe and healthy work environment for all employees.

Employees are expected to follow safety guidelines and report any hazards or unsafe conditions. The company will take appropriate measures to address such issues.

Workspaces should be maintained in a clean and organized manner. Shared resources should be used responsibly and returned to their designated locations.

Any incidents affecting employee health or safety must be reported immediately to the operations team.

## 42. Diversity and Equal Opportunity

The company is committed to providing equal employment opportunities to all individuals, regardless of background.

Decisions related to hiring, promotion, and compensation are based on merit and business needs. Discrimination of any kind is not tolerated.

Employees are expected to contribute to an inclusive and respectful workplace environment.

This handbook is a living document and may be updated periodically to reflect changes in company practices, legal requirements, or organizational needs.

Employees will be notified of significant updates. Continued employment constitutes acceptance of updated policies.

Suggestions for improvements to the handbook may be submitted to the operations team for consideration.
