## 1. Introduction

Sagarmatha Systems Pvt. Ltd. is a Nepal-based technology company focused on building scalable software infrastructure, enterprise-grade applications, and data systems for regional and international clients. This handbook is intended to provide employees with a clear understanding of how the organization operates, what is expected of them, and what they can expect in return.

This document is not a contract of employment. It is a reference framework that defines policies, processes, and operational norms. In situations where interpretation is required, final authority rests with the executive leadership team.

Employees are expected to read this handbook in full during their onboarding period and revisit it when needed. Updates are made periodically, and employees will be notified of significant changes.

---

## 2. Company Background

Sagarmatha Systems was formally incorporated on 12 January 2021 under the laws of Nepal. The company began operations on 01 March 2021 with a small engineering team focused on backend systems and infrastructure tooling.

The founding team consists of Aarav Shrestha (Chief Executive Officer), Milan Karki (Chief Technology Officer), and Prakriti Bhandari (Chief Operating Officer). The company was established with the objective of building reliable and scalable systems that function effectively within the infrastructural constraints common in South Asia.

Since its founding, the company has expanded its capabilities to include frontend systems, data engineering, and applied machine learning. The organization serves a mix of local enterprises and international clients, with a primary focus on long-term system reliability rather than short-term feature velocity.

---

## 3. Mission, Vision, and Values

The mission of Sagarmatha Systems is to design and operate software systems that are reliable, maintainable, and adaptable to real-world constraints. The company prioritizes long-term stability and clarity over rapid but unsustainable growth.

The long-term vision is to become a leading provider of infrastructure and systems engineering solutions originating from Nepal, with a reputation for technical depth and operational reliability.

The company operates on a set of core values that guide decision-making across teams. These values are not aspirational statements but practical principles used in daily work.

Clarity is prioritized over speed. Decisions should be understood before they are executed, even if that requires additional time. Ownership is expected at all levels. Employees are responsible for the outcomes of their work, not just the completion of assigned tasks. Simplicity is preferred over unnecessary complexity. Systems should be designed to be understood and maintained by others, not just their original authors. Finally, consistency in behavior and decision-making is valued over occasional excellence.

## 4. Organizational Structure

Sagarmatha Systems operates with a functional organizational structure, divided into Engineering, Product, Operations, and Support functions. While reporting lines are clearly defined, cross-functional collaboration is expected and encouraged.

The executive leadership team consists of the CEO, CTO, and COO. Strategic decisions, including major investments, hiring expansions, and product direction, are made at this level.

Engineering is led by the Director of Engineering, who oversees multiple teams including Platform Engineering, Product Engineering, and Data Systems. Each team is managed by an Engineering Manager responsible for delivery, team development, and technical alignment.

Operations includes human resources, finance, and administrative functions. The COO oversees this department, ensuring that organizational processes are efficient and compliant with legal requirements.

Support functions, including Quality Assurance and Customer Support, operate in coordination with Engineering and Product teams to ensure system reliability and user satisfaction.

The company maintains multiple office locations within Nepal to support its operations.

The headquarters is located on the fourth floor of Laxmi Plaza, Kamaladi Road, Kathmandu. This office serves as the central administrative and executive hub, housing leadership, operations, and a portion of the engineering team.

A secondary engineering office is located in Pulchowk, Lalitpur, at House No. 18/3. This office is primarily used by backend and infrastructure teams due to its proximity to technical institutions and a larger engineering talent pool.

Employees are assigned a primary office location but may work from other offices with prior approval from their manager.

## 6. Employment Categories

Employees at Sagarmatha Systems are classified into several categories based on their role and contractual arrangement.

Full-time employees work standard hours and are eligible for all benefits outlined in this handbook. Part-time employees work reduced hours and receive prorated benefits where applicable.

Contract employees are engaged for specific projects or durations and are governed by separate contractual agreements. Interns are engaged for learning and development purposes, typically for a duration of three to six months.

Each category has clearly defined expectations and compensation structures, which are communicated at the time of hiring.

---

## 7. Working Hours and Attendance

The standard working hours are from 9:30 AM to 6:30 PM, Sunday through Friday, with Saturday designated as a weekly holiday. Employees are expected to be present during core collaboration hours, which run from 11:00 AM to 4:00 PM.

A flexible arrival window of up to 45 minutes is permitted, provided that employees complete their required working hours and maintain productivity.

Attendance is tracked through the company’s internal system. Repeated late arrivals or unapproved absences may result in corrective action, depending on frequency and impact.

Employees are expected to inform their manager in advance if they anticipate being late or absent. In cases of emergency, notification should be provided as soon as reasonably possible.

---

## 8. Leave Policy

Sagarmatha Systems provides a structured leave policy to ensure that employees can take time off while maintaining operational continuity.

Annual leave is provided at a rate of 18 days per year. Leave accrues monthly and may be carried forward up to a maximum of 7 days into the following year. Any unused leave beyond this limit will expire.

Sick leave is provided at 12 days per year. Employees are required to provide a medical certificate if sick leave exceeds two consecutive days.

Casual leave is limited to 6 days per year and is intended for short, unplanned absences. Casual leave cannot be combined with annual leave for extended periods.

Maternity leave is provided for a duration of 98 days, in compliance with Nepali labor law. Paternity leave is provided for 15 days.

Unpaid leave may be granted under special circumstances, subject to approval by the employee’s manager and the COO.

Leave requests must be submitted through the internal system and approved in advance, except in cases of emergency.

## 9. Compensation and Salary Structure

Sagarmatha Systems maintains a structured salary framework based on role, experience, and performance. Salary bands are reviewed periodically to remain competitive within the local and regional market.

Compensation is paid on a monthly basis, with payments processed on the last working day of each month. Employees are responsible for ensuring that their banking information is accurate.

In addition to base salary, employees may be eligible for performance-based increments, bonuses, and other incentives as determined by management.

The company aims to maintain internal equity while recognizing individual contributions and market conditions.

---

## 10. Salary Review Process

Salary reviews are conducted twice a year, typically in March and September. The process begins with a self-assessment, followed by a manager evaluation and peer feedback.

Managers assess employees based on performance, contribution to team objectives, and alignment with company values. Final decisions are made during calibration meetings to ensure consistency across teams.

Salary adjustments take effect in April and October, following the completion of the review cycle.

Employees are encouraged to discuss their performance and career progression with their manager regularly, rather than waiting for formal review cycles.

---

## 11. Performance Management

Performance management at Sagarmatha Systems is designed to provide ongoing feedback and support employee growth. Evaluations consider multiple factors, including technical execution, problem-solving ability, communication, and ownership.

Performance discussions are conducted at least twice a year, but informal feedback is expected to occur more frequently. Managers are responsible for providing clear and actionable feedback.

Employees who consistently meet or exceed expectations may be considered for promotions or expanded responsibilities. Those who do not meet expectations may be placed on a structured improvement plan.

The goal of performance management is not only to evaluate past performance but also to guide future development.

---

## 12. Code of Conduct

All employees are expected to maintain a professional and respectful work environment. Behavior that is discriminatory, harassing, or otherwise inappropriate will not be tolerated.

Employees are expected to communicate clearly and respectfully, both in person and through digital channels. Differences of opinion should be addressed constructively.

Confidential information must be handled with care. Employees should not share sensitive company or client information without authorization.

Violations of the code of conduct may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

---

## 13. Disciplinary Procedures

Disciplinary actions are taken when employees fail to meet performance or behavioral expectations. The process is designed to be fair and consistent.

Initial concerns are typically addressed through informal discussions between the employee and their manager. If issues persist, a formal warning may be issued.

In cases where improvement is required, a Performance Improvement Plan may be implemented. This plan outlines specific goals, timelines, and support measures.

Failure to meet the requirements of a Performance Improvement Plan may result in further disciplinary action, including termination of employment.

---

## 14. Technology Stack Overview

Sagarmatha Systems uses a modern technology stack designed for scalability and maintainability. Backend systems are primarily developed using Go, with Node.js used for certain services where appropriate.

Frontend applications are built using React and TypeScript, providing a consistent and maintainable user interface framework.

Infrastructure is managed using Kubernetes, with deployments hosted on cloud platforms in the Asia region. PostgreSQL serves as the primary database, with Redis used for caching and performance optimization.

The selection of technologies is guided by performance requirements, team expertise, and long-term maintainability.

---

## 15. Security and Compliance

Security is a critical aspect of all systems developed and operated by Sagarmatha Systems. Employees are required to follow established security practices, including the use of strong passwords and two-factor authentication.

Access to systems and data is granted based on role and necessity. Employees should not attempt to access information beyond their authorized scope.

Regular audits and reviews are conducted to ensure compliance with security standards. Any suspected security issues must be reported immediately.

## 16. Communication and Tools

The company uses a set of standardized tools for communication and collaboration. Slack is used for internal communication, Notion for documentation, and Jira for task management.

Employees are expected to use these tools consistently and maintain clear records of decisions and discussions.

Important decisions should be documented in a persistent format to ensure transparency and continuity.
